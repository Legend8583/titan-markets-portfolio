Macro NOTE-001

Rate Expectations & Sector Rotation

rates rotation macro

üìä Overview

This research examines the relationship between Federal Reserve policy shifts and sector relative strength, with specific focus on how rate expectations drive capital rotation between cyclical and defensive sectors.

Key Insight: Historical analysis shows that anticipation of rate changes drives sector rotation 3-6 months before actual policy implementation, creating actionable trading opportunities in relative value strategies.

üåç Macro Context

Current Environment

The Federal Reserve's messaging around terminal rates and the path forward has significant implications for sector allocation. As rate expectations shift, we observe predictable patterns in sector performance:

  • Rising Rate Expectations: Favor Financials (XLF), Energy (XLE), Materials (XLB)
  • Falling Rate Expectations: Favor Utilities (XLP), Real Estate (XLRE), Consumer Staples (XLP)
  • Rate Uncertainty: Favor Healthcare (XLV), Tech (XLK) with strong FCF

Historical Context

Examining the last 3 rate cycles (2015-2018, 2019, 2022-2024) reveals consistent patterns:

  • Financials outperform during rising rate environment (+12-15% relative to SPY)
  • Utilities underperform during tightening cycles (-8-12% relative to SPY)
  • Tech rotation depends on growth vs. value dynamics within sector

üî¨ Analytical Framework

Correlation Analysis

We track rolling 60-day correlations between sector ETFs and key rate indicators:

  • 10-Year Treasury Yield (proxy for long-term rates)
  • 2-Year Treasury Yield (proxy for Fed policy expectations)
  • 2s10s Spread (yield curve steepness)
  • Fed Funds Futures (implied policy path)

Relative Strength Metrics

Key indicators for identifying rotation opportunities:

  • Sector/SPY ratio momentum (20, 50, 200-day MAs)
  • Breadth divergences within sectors (advancing vs. declining stocks)
  • Volume-weighted relative strength index
  • Option flow and positioning (skew, put/call ratios)

üí° Trading Implications

Long/Short Pairs

Implement rate-driven rotation through sector pairs trades:

  • Rising Rates: Long XLF / Short XLU (Financials vs. Utilities)
  • Falling Rates: Long XLU / Short XLF (Utilities vs. Financials)
  • Steepening Curve: Long XLF / Short XLB (Banks benefit from steep curve)

Timing Considerations

Optimal entry timing based on Fed communication and data releases:

  • Position ahead of FOMC meetings (2-3 weeks prior)
  • Adjust after CPI/PCE releases (inflation expectations shifts)
  • Monitor Fed speakers for policy pivot signals
  • Track positioning via CFTC Commitment of Traders data

  • Policy Uncertainty: Fed communication can be inconsistent or data-dependent
  • Correlation Breakdown: Historical relationships may not hold in unique environments
  • Sector-Specific News: Idiosyncratic events can override macro themes
  • Global Factors: ECB, BOJ policies can influence US sector dynamics
  • Liquidity Conditions: QT/QE programs affect sector performance independently
